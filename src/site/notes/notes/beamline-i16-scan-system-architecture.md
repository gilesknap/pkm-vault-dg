Whiteboard diagram captured at Diamond Light Source describing the control-flow and software/hardware architecture for a synchrotron scattering experiment on [[beamline-i16\|beamline-i16]]. The overarching goal is detecting **non-dominant (weak) signals** in scattering data — annotated on the board as *"General: i16 interested in non-dom signals"*.

## PhD Research Block

A red-dashed box labelled *PhD* contains the upstream research pipeline:

- **Symmetry modelling** — characterising the expected symmetry of the sample.
- **Measurement / signal simulation of candidate** — simulating predicted scattering from candidate structures.
- **Choose projection for efficient elimination** — selecting the reciprocal-space projection that most efficiently rules out candidate structures.

This feeds into the scan planning pipeline. Related concept: [[reciprocal-space\|reciprocal-space]] and [[ewald-sphere\|ewald-sphere]] geometry (sketched top-right on the board).

## Space Checks

### Physical space checks
- **Lipid/tia space limits** — motor travel bounds.
- **Collision checks** — ensuring no mechanical clash during the proposed scan trajectory.

### Virtual space checks
- **Other dominant scattering processes** — identifying and suppressing background signals.
- **Multiple Scattering** — accounting for second-order diffraction paths.
- **Diffuse Multiple Scattering** — handling incoherent/diffuse contributions.

Physical and virtual checks are bidirectional and feed into the resolution validation stage.

## Resolution Validation State Machine

A state machine waits until the proposed scan passes all checks (*"watches until ready, else retry"*). Once validated it proceeds to the scan execution loop.

## Scan Controller Loop

The bottom loop (red-dashed) describes hardware execution:

1. **Controller** — [[fpga\|fpga]], [[pmac\|pmac]], or [[geobrick\|geobrick]] motion controller.
2. **Perform scan** — execute the trajectory.
3. **Decouple** — detach hardware locks / triggers.
4. **Rise** — reset/park motors.
5. **Data Analysis → Fin!!** — results fed back upstream.

The board also notes *"Create Trig. UI knowledge of 6003 spaces"*, referring to the [[notes/tech-ui-notes\|trigonometry UI]] needed to reason over the six-circle (6003) diffractometer geometry.
